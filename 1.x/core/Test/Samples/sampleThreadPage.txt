Note the significance of Kakashi's main summoned dog using the same shampoo as Sakura.<br />

                <br />

                Compare and contrast these bystanders with Akamaru, who seems to truly have agency in his own story. Akamaru decides when, how and where to pee, and his growth symbolizes the growing presence and importance of dogs vs. the waning power of the frogs, snails and snakes. This latter theme has subtle manifestations throughout the manga, though by far the most obvious are the death of the legendary three and the failure of the new generation to follow in their footsteps (Sakura has not summoned anything at all, and Sasuke seems to be all about hawks now).<br />

                <br />

                Given these themes, it only makes sense that Naruto will ultimately realize that Sakura is not the one for him, and transfer his feelings to Kiba's sister.<br />

                <br />

                Crap, we're back to shipping.
